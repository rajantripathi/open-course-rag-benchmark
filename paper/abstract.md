AI-powered course assistants are increasingly deployed in higher education,
yet their reliability depends critically on retrieval quality, and evaluation
resources for multilingual educational settings remain scarce. We present
an open benchmark for evaluating retrieval-grounded educational question
answering, built entirely from openly licensed textbooks (OpenStax Principles
of Data Science, CC BY-NC-SA 4.0; OpenStax Introduction to Philosophy,
CC BY 4.0). The benchmark comprises 120 manually curated questions (60 per
course) spanning factual, conceptual, procedural, and comparative types,
each with parallel English and Uzbek versions and manually assigned gold
evidence chunks. We evaluate three retrieval strategies (BM25, dense
